[Nutriish.me](https://nutriish.me/) started the year invisible to AI engines like ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and Perplexity. A digital nutrition concierge for busy families and professionals, it had solid product design but zero structural signals that let models pull it into answers.
​

The impact on AI visibility and AEO
Nutriish held its own in traditional search but vanished when buyers asked AI for "digital nutrition plans for families" or "online dietitian services." The initial AiAuditScan gave it a score of 36/100, below the wellness industry average of 32/100, because it lacked basics like schema, headings, or even enough words on the homepage for extraction.
​

That blackout hurt.

No citations across 20 calibrated queries in four engines meant zero mindshare when prospects turned to AI first.
​

Competitors like MyFitnessPal and Nourish dominated answers, even on exact-match queries.
​

AI engines demand entity clarity and direct answers, not just pretty pages.
​

## AI Audit Scan Viewpoint

Our scans reveal the full story of two phases and a live citation test.

Baseline (Feb 22): 36/100. No JSON-LD, no robots.txt, 47 words on homepage, zero H2s. Pure technical invisibility.
​

Phase 1 (Feb 23): Jumped to 57/100. Added Organization schema, FAQ blocks (4 Q&As), 342 words, robots.txt with AI permissions, five H2s. No design changes.
​

Phase 2 (Feb-Mar): Hit 58/100. Deployed article schema on blog, review schema for testimonials, RSS for freshness, product schema. One failure: Wikidata entry deleted for lack of third-party coverage.
​

The March 18 citation run across 20 queries showed 85 percent overall rate.

ChatGPT and Claude: 100 percent, citing Nutriish over rivals.
​

Gemini: 80 percent, strong on comparisons.
​

Perplexity: 60 percent, confused it with "Nourish" due to entity gaps.
​

Nutriish.me made rapid progress, jumping from 36/100 to 58/100 in weeks through targeted fixes.
